Creative Time and Weeksville Heritage Center present Funk, God, Jazz, and Medicine: Black Radical Brooklyn, a walkable month-long art exhibition of four community-based art commissions, one of which is OJBK Radio.


OJBK Radio

Artist collective Otabenga Jones & Associates is collaborating with the Central Brooklyn Jazz Consortium to produce a temporary outdoor radio station that will broadcast live from the back of a pink 1959 Cadillac Coupe de Ville. Broadcasts will pay tribute to former Bed-Stuy cultural center "The East," founded in 1969 as a hub for creating cultural awareness around the Black Nationalism and pan-Africanist movements.


Otabenga Jones & Associates

Otabenga Jones & Associates is a Houston-based educational art organization founded in 2002 by members Dawolu Jabari Anderson, Jamal Cyrus, Kenya Evans, and Robert A. Pruitt, among others. The collective--which creates actions, writings and installations, exploring the transatlantic experiences of the African Diaspora--is named in tribute to Ota Benga, the Congolese Pygmy man who sought refuge at Weeksville's Howard Colored Orphan Asylum in Brooklyn after being exhibited in 1906 at the Bronx Zoo.
